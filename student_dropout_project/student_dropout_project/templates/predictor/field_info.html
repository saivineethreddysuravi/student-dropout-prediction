Field Information & Descriptions

This page explains each input field used in the Student Dropout Prediction form. Use it as a reference when entering data.

1. Student & Admission Details
  • Marital status – Student’s marital status at enrollment (Single, Married, Widower, Divorced, Facto union, Legally separated).
  • Application mode – Type of admission route used (e.g., 1st phase general contingent, transfer, international student, etc.).
  • Application order – Preference for this course: 0 = 1st choice, 9 = 10th (last) choice.
  • Course – Degree program in which the student is enrolled (e.g., Informatics Engineering, Nursing, Tourism, Management, etc.).
  • Daytime/evening attendance – Whether the student attends daytime or evening classes.
  • Previous qualification – Highest education level completed before entering this course (secondary, bachelor, master, etc.).
  • Previous qualification grade – Final grade from the previous qualification on a 0–200 scale.
  • Nationality – Student’s nationality (Portuguese, Brazilian, Romanian, etc.).
  • Mother’s/Father’s qualification – Highest education level completed by each parent.
  • Mother’s/Father’s occupation – Main occupation group for each parent (e.g., administrative staff, health professionals, unskilled workers).
  • Admission grade – Admission score (0–200) used by the university when accepting the student.
  • Displaced – Whether the student is living away from their home region to attend the course.
  • Educational special needs – Indicates if the student has registered educational special needs.
  • Debtor – Whether the student is in debt to the institution (e.g., unpaid fees).
  • Tuition fees up to date – Whether all tuition payments are currently up to date.
  • Gender – Student gender (Male/Female in this dataset).
  • Scholarship holder – Whether the student receives a scholarship.
  • Age at enrollment – Age (in years) at the moment of enrollment.
  • International – Whether the student is classified as an international student.
2. Academic Performance (1st Semester)
  • Curricular units 1st sem credited – Number of subjects for which credit was granted in the 1st semester (no classes attended). Typical range: 0–20.
  • Curricular units 1st sem enrolled – Number of subjects the student enrolled in during the 1st semester. Typical range: 0–26.
  • Curricular units 1st sem evaluations – Number of evaluations (tests/exams) the student actually attended in the 1st semester. Range: 0–45.
  • Curricular units 1st sem approved – Number of subjects passed in the 1st semester. Range: 0–26.
  • Curricular units 1st sem grade – Average grade in the 1st semester on a 0–20 scale.
  • Curricular units 1st sem without evaluations – Number of subjects where the student did not attend any evaluation in the 1st semester. Range: 0–12.
3. Academic Performance (2nd Semester)
  • Curricular units 2nd sem credited – Number of subjects credited in the 2nd semester. Range: 0–19.
  • Curricular units 2nd sem enrolled – Number of subjects enrolled in the 2nd semester. Range: 0–23.
  • Curricular units 2nd sem evaluations – Number of evaluations attended in the 2nd semester. Range: 0–33.
  • Curricular units 2nd sem approved – Number of subjects passed in the 2nd semester. Range: 0–20.
  • Curricular units 2nd sem grade – Average grade in the 2nd semester on a 0–20 scale.
  • Curricular units 2nd sem without evaluations – Number of subjects without any evaluation in the 2nd semester. Range: 0–12.
4. Economic Context
  • Unemployment rate – National unemployment rate (%) at the time of the semester. Typical values in the dataset: 7.6–16.2.
  • Inflation rate – National inflation rate (%) at the time. Typical values: -0.8–3.7.
  • GDP – GDP growth rate or index for the country at the time. Values in the dataset are between approximately -4.06 and 3.51.

Tip: When filling the prediction form, you can keep this page open in another tab for quick reference, or click the “Field info” link from the prediction page.
